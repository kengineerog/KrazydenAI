About KrazyDen AI

The Philosophy of Krazy Intelligence

KrazyDen AI isn't just another tech project; it's a rebellion against the "Black Box" of modern artificial intelligence. We believe that true intelligence shouldn't be locked behind massive server farms or corporate filters. It should be wild, efficient, and open.

"We aren't building just an AI; we're building a new way for silicon to think—from the smallest Raspberry Pi to the most complex robot."

Our mission is to achieve Reasoning at the Edge. By optimizing every single parameter, we enable high-level logic (CoT) to run on devices that other models wouldn't even boot on. KrazyDen is built from the ground up to be uncensored, lightweight, and aggressively innovative.

Why "Krazy"?

Because everyone said it was impossible to run Chain-of-Thought reasoning on a micro-controller. We did it anyway. Because we value transparency and experimentation over safe, boring algorithms. We embrace the chaos of rapid iteration to find the next breakthrough.

The Creator

Kanishk Sampathkumar

Hi, I'm Kanishk. I'm the lead architect and founder behind KrazyDen AI. I started this journey because I was tired of the limitations placed on modern AI—the high costs, the lack of privacy, and the massive hardware requirements.

My background is rooted in deep system optimization and neural architecture. I don't just want to build AI that talks; I want to build AI that understands how to interact with the world around it—whether that's through a Linux terminal or a robotic arm.

"The goal has always been simple: empower the individual. Give everyone the power of a digital brain that they own, control, and can run anywhere."

Current Focus

Right now, I'm obsessively refining the KrazyKoreDen engine, pushing our 12% model completion toward a full-scale release by 2026. When I'm not coding neural layers, I'm usually figuring out how to squeeze more performance out of Ubuntu-based edge devices.
